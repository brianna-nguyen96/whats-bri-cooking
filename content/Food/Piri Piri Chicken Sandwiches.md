Recipe from The Wishbone Kitchen Cookbook

![[FC0D8F71-9E8A-4AC6-B5D2-D1C4646F4D5E_1_105_c.jpeg|center|363]]

I would say that my current relationship with TikTok is neutral, but in the depths of the pandemic I was just as susceptible as anyone to doomscrolling. I’m not sure exactly when Wishbone Kitchen came onto my feed, but Meredith Hayden sort of came and went, a perfect symbol of aspirational, East coast, extremely White luxury that’s so foreign to me.

Chris and I joke about my emotional support white women, namely Alison Roman - I just think I was in the perfect time and place for Bon Appetit/California-coded food content in the mid-2010s. I remember reading about Alison Roman’s signature orange-y red nail polish color, Essie Clambake. Unfortunately, Clambake was discontinued in 2017, so I was never able to get my hands on it. For the next 8 years, I occasionally checked online for dupes, but nothing ever matched up at the drugstore. Until one day last year, I saw that Clambake was being brought back by Essie to promote the release of The Wishbone Kitchen Cookbook. I couldn’t believe my luck, and immediately placed an order at Ulta. In my appreciation of Meredith’s generosity in resurrecting a discontinued, cult nail polish I checked out a copy of The Wishbone Kitchen Cookbook.

The piri-piri chicken sandwiches were one of the first recipes that I bookmarked. Nando’s had just opened their first location in Austin, and the wait times were still outrageous. For the record, I think Nando’s is fine! I just don’t think it’s worth it to wait outside in the Austin heat for the chance to eat grilled chicken from a chain. The first time I made these sandwiches I used boneless, skinless chicken thighs, which is what Meredith calls for in the original recipe. This week, chicken breast was on sale at Vons, so in the name of cost savings and gainz, I decided to swap chicken parts.

You start off by charring some red bell pepper and jalapeño, letting the peppers sit for a bit in a covered plastic bag, and then peeling the skins off. Peeling the skin off a jalapeño was simple enough, but both times I’ve made this recipe I’ve had so much trouble peeling the bell pepper skin… It is pretty forgiving, though - everything eventually goes in the food processor to create the marinade. The marinade also includes the namesake piri-piri peppers, which I’ve never been able to find at Central Market, HEB, or Vons. Meredith recommends red pepper flakes as a substitute, which has worked well for me. (I do wonder what it would taste like with the actual piri-piri though, so I may have to do an update to this post one day.) After pureeing the peppers with lemon juice, cilantro, paprika, sugar, cayenne and olive oil, you’re left with this nice terra-cotta marinade. We used half the marinade to marinate the chicken, and the other half to use as sauce on the sandwich.

![[5A27C183-96B5-4D93-8610-1CCCB60B18E8_1_105_c.jpeg|center||363]]

The rest of the recipe is pretty straightforward, you cook the chicken, dress some arugula in olive oil, and assemble it all together with the sauce on a brioche bun. I added some avocado to add some extra fat to the chicken breast, which I think was a good call. The chicken breast was still juicy and nicely marinated, but unfortunately the chicken thigh iteration of this recipe was still better. I do enjoy this recipe, but I’ll stick to the written recipe in the future. I wouldn’t call this any sort of Nando’s dupe, but it’s a nice little sandwich that’s worth the extra prep time.
